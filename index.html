Publications

Memory-Efficient Deep Learning Architectures for Water Body Segmentation in Satellite Imagery: A Comparative Analysis and Ensemble Approach

Accepted — 28th ICCIT, 2025

This paper presents a comparative study of memory-efficient deep learning architectures for water body segmentation from satellite imagery. An ensemble-based approach is proposed to balance accuracy and computational efficiency, making the model suitable for resource-constrained environments.

Transformer-Based Multi-Label Disease Prediction from Radiology Reports

Accepted — 28th ICCIT, 2025

This work explores transformer-based natural language processing models for multi-label disease prediction from unstructured radiology reports. The study demonstrates the effectiveness of attention-based architectures in extracting clinical insights from medical text data.
